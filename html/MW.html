How to get here

The Canary Islands are the southernmost point of Europe. There are many flights daily that connect the islands to most of the EU countries and Russia within a few hours.


Visa

If you are a Russian citizen you must show a valid, current visa. A multivisa valid for 180 days is issued for a standard tourist trip.


Border control

If you travel to the islands from a country within the Schengen area (most EU countries) you should not have to pass any kind of control on arrival at the airport. Passengers arriving from any country not belonging to this area, such as Great Britain and Ireland, must go through a passport control on arrival at the Canary Islands airports.


If you are planning to visit the Canary Islands, you should be aware that:

If you are a citizen of the EU, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland and Liechtenstein you only need to show a valid passport or national ID card on arrival at the airport.


Ways to travel in Tenerife

There are many ways to travel in Tenerife.
You can rent bikes to ride around the urban landscape and mountain trails. Or maybe you want to travel with public transport and feel the local daily life?
Travelling with friends or family? You can rent a car at airports or in the city. Also if you want to see the others islands too you can rent a boat or use ferry.

Passports are always required except for citizens of the European Union, Switzerland, United Kingdom, or countries that are members of the European Economic Area. In these cases, citizens can travel with a valid identity card (except for minors, who must have a parental permit).


We recommend that you arrange your rental vehicle before you travel and collect it at the airport upon arrival, at many train and bus stations or at the office agreed with the rental company. You can also book it directly at your destination. You can find car rental companies at all the airports and in the main train and bus stations.


If you need to change your money to €, you will be able to do so at bureaus de change, at various banks, and at the majority of hotels and travel agencies. You will normally find offices or ATMs that exchange currency in the international airports and high speed train stations.


Bus journeys are usually paid for upon climbing aboard and they usually only accept cash. Payment is always in € and change is usually limited (never above €20). Metro and train journeys are paid for at the station and there are usually ticket machines. On certain occasions, they only accept payment by credit or debit card.


“112” is the free emergency phone number and is always available and with teletranslation in several languages. Remember it should only be used in cases of emergency. You can also find a presence of the law enforcement bodies in the airports, ports, and international train and bus stations. You can go to the National Police and National Guard if needed. If your problem is related to your transport or luggage, we recommend that you firstly contact the company responsible for your travel. The mobile app Alertcops will also allow you to communicate directly with law enforcement authorities and alert them of any abnormal situation or violence. It is available in English, French, German, Italian, Russian and Spanish.
